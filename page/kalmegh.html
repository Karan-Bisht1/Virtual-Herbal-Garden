Kalmegh

Andrographis paniculata, commonly known as Kalmegh or "King of Bitters," is a highly valued medicinal herb in the Unani, Siddha, and Homeopathy systems. It is renowned for its hepatoprotective, anti-inflammatory, and immune-boosting properties.

Medicinal Uses

Kalmegh is used in the following systems:

  • Unani: Used for treating fevers, liver disorders, and digestive issues.
  • Siddha: Known as "Nilavembu," it is used for treating viral fevers, arthritis, and skin diseases.
  • Homeopathy: Used in tinctures to address chronic liver and digestive problems.

Home Remedies

  • Kalmegh Tea: A herbal infusion made with dried leaves is used to improve liver health and boost immunity.
  • Liver Tonic: Kalmegh powder mixed with honey can be taken to detoxify the liver and manage jaundice.
  • Skin Paste: A paste of Kalmegh leaves is applied to wounds for faster healing and to reduce inflammation.

Other Benefits

  • Anti-Malarial: Kalmegh is used for treating malaria and other febrile conditions.
  • Immune Boosting: Regular consumption helps enhance the body's immune response to infections.
  • Anti-Cancer Potential: Research suggests its extracts may have cancer-fighting properties.
  • Environmental Use: Acts as a natural pest repellent in agricultural practices.
